Building This Website in Under an Hour with AI

I built this website in under an hour using Claude 3.7! This tech isn't exactly new—I've heard about AI-assisted web development for a while—but a friend finally inspired me to test it out.

There are plenty of AI website builders out there, but I chose Claude because it could pull directly from my resume to generate most of the content. And honestly? It was remarkably simple. The end result looks far more polished than what I've seen from some other LLMs I've tested (cough OpenAI cough).

Why This is a Game-Changer for Operators

Having worked on web development projects a few times in my career, I wish this technology had existed sooner. The implications for a 0-to-1 startup environment are massive:

  • ✅ Bridging the gap between technical & non-technical teams – As a non-technical operator, I can now articulate my vision more clearly when working with web developers.
  • ✅ Faster prototyping – Instead of waiting weeks for a first draft, I can spin up a starting point myself and hand over a working concept.
  • ✅ Iterating with AI – The ability to tweak content, refine designs, and even experiment with different messaging makes this a powerful tool for early-stage companies.

Limitations & Lessons Learned

Of course, this wasn't a perfect, one-click solution. I ran into a few roadblocks:

  • 🔄 Prompt repetition – I had to rephrase and refine prompts multiple times to debug issues and adjust the content/design.
  • 🎨 Tone & aesthetics adjustments – Getting Claude to fully understand the exact look and feel I wanted took a bit of back-and-forth.
  • 🚧 80/20 rule applies – Like most AI tools today, this got me to 80% very quickly, but it still required hands-on fine-tuning to reach 100%.

Final Thoughts

AI-powered web development isn't replacing designers or engineers anytime soon, but it's an incredible accelerator—especially for non-technical folks like me. Whether it's for rapid prototyping, content drafting, or just making conversations with dev teams more productive, tools like this are changing the game.

Would I use AI to build a full-scale website from scratch? Probably not. Would I use it again for quick projects, MVPs, or internal tools? Absolutely.

LillyBot: A Custom Chatbot for My Dog

I Built a Chatbot For My Dog—And It's Changing How I Define 10x Operators 🐶💼

Imagine this—you're on a flight to your best friend's wedding and your dog sitter texts you "HELP! What medication does Lilly need tonight?" Instead of panicking, you simply send a link to a custom chatbot that answers any questions about your pet's care.

That's exactly what I built with LillyBot—a custom chatbot that talks like my dog (yes, with plenty of "woofs" and "hooman" references) and handles all care questions.

Imagine this—you're on a flight to your best friend's wedding and your dog sitter texts you "HELP! What medication does Lilly need tonight?" Instead of panicking, you simply send a link to a custom chatbot that answers any questions about your pet's care.

That's exactly what I built with LillyBot—a custom chatbot that talks like my dog (yes, with plenty of "woofs" and "hooman" references) and handles all care questions.

🚀 How It Works: LillyBot pulls info from a custom Google Doc representing the simplest form of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)—so instead of ChatGPT making up answers, it references exact details I've written while still reasoning like an AI.

Where This Gets Interesting for Workplaces

This was a fun personal project, but the implications for productivity are massive:

  • ✅ Scaling Expert Knowledge – People managers and HR teams can embed chatbots trained on internal documentation to provide employees with instant answers instead of digging through wikis or Slack threads.
  • ✅ AI Concierge for Hospitality – Airbnb hosts, boutique hotels, and vendors can create a chatbot for check-ins, recommendations, and guest services.
  • ✅ Easily Shareable & Scalable – Unlike a ChatGPT project that stays tied to one user, external chatbot links make these tools accessible across teams without login barriers.
  • ✅ Power of Simple RAG – LillyBot is just scratching the surface. This simple implementation with one document shows why companies are building complex RAG systems with multiple knowledge sources and sophisticated knowledge graphs.
  • ✅ The True Enterprise Potential – When you move from my single Google Doc to connecting entire document repositories, Slack conversations, and domain-specific databases, you unlock exponentially more powerful AI assistants that can reason across your entire org's knowledge base.

What Didn't Work (Yet)

  • ❌ Still only as good as the inputs – If my Google Doc is incomplete, LillyBot can't fill in the gaps. This applies to any AI assistant—garbage in, garbage out.
  • ❌ Not fully interactive – It answers sitter questions well, but it's not yet smart enough to proactively guide them. Future iterations could push reminders (e.g., "It's dinner time! Feed me 🦴").

Final Thoughts

This experiment reinforced something key: AI assistants don't replace people—they augment them. If you feed them structured, reliable knowledge, they can be a force multiplier in both personal and professional settings.

Would I rely on an AI to fully replace the personal human touch of a manager or a concierge? No. Would I use this chatbot to 10x my productivity? 100%.
